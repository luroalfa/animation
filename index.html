My Animations

As part of the learning process about animations, these are some of my exercises applied in this module.


1) Objective:


The first exercise is about to create an animation with at least three principles of animation. In this case, I made an animation at After Effects about how to bounce the ball. In this image gif is a simple exercise, we can see the following principles of animation the first principle is squash and stretch.


This is the principle that gives flexibility to objects, we can see as the ball starts to fall and picks up speed, the ball will stretch out just before impact as the ball impacts the floor, it squashes before stretching again as it takes off. When it is squashed or stretched, the width and depth need to correspond accordingly.


The second principle is the ease in and ease out, this principle is about having a time for acceleration and deceleration of an object. Without ease in and ease out, movements become very unnatural and robotic. That's why the ball has a smooth exit up and a smooth entry down in the movements up of the ball.


And last but not least, we have the third principle applied, which is about the secondary action. Every animation has to have a secondary action to support or emphasize the main action to breathe more life into the animation and create a more convincing performance. It's important to remember that the secondary action should typically be something subtle that doesn't detract from the main action happening.


In my exercise, you can find the secondary action as the shadow where get large and shrinks, also its color is more intense when the ball hits the floor and it's less intense when it moves away from the floor.


Challenge:


My challenge in this project was to apply the ease in and ease out at the ball. Because I didn't know how to do it, and finally I learned to apply it through some tutorial videos.


Technologies:


For this project, I used After Effects to make it.
